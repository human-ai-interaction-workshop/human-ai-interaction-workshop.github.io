Topics of Interest

The workshop focuses on the science of interaction: how humans and AI systems co-adapt over time, and how to design, train, and evaluate interactive AI responsibly.

Theory & Measurement
  • Formal models of human–AI interaction and co-adaptation
  • Trust, reliance, calibration, and decision quality
  • Cognitive effort, usability, and human factors as objectives
  • Longitudinal / multi-turn outcomes and guarantees
Algorithms & Learning
  • Learning objectives for interaction and assistive decision-making
  • Feedback, preference modeling, and alignment under interaction
  • Calibration, robustness, uncertainty, and abstention policies
  • Personalization and adaptation with safety constraints
Benchmarks & Applications
  • Interactive benchmarks, protocols, and evaluation harnesses
  • Human-in-the-loop learning and decision support systems
  • Agents/assistants in real workflows (health, education, science, etc.)
  • Responsible deployment: auditing, transparency, and governance
