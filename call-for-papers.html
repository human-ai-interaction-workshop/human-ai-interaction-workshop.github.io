Call for Papers

We invite submissions on the foundations of human–AI interaction: theory, algorithms, and practice. This page is the detailed CFP; the homepage has a brief overview.

Back to overview

Topics of Interest

We aim to bring together researchers working across theory, algorithms, and practice to develop a shared language and tools for studying human–AI interaction. We invite contributions along three tightly connected dimensions:

Theory & Measurement

Foundational principles and formal models of human–AI interaction, including:

  • Statistical learning theory for interaction
  • Uncertainty quantification and decision making with humans in the loop
  • Value of information in collaborative settings
  • Game-theoretic and sequential models of collaboration
  • Theoretical characterizations of human responses and behavior
  • Trust, reliance, calibration, and decision quality
  • Cognitive effort, usability, and human factors as formal objectives
  • Longitudinal and multi-turn outcome guarantees
Algorithms & Learning

Algorithmic methods that operationalize foundational interaction principles, such as:

  • Learning with human feedback (RLHF and beyond)
  • Interactive and conversational systems
  • Uncertainty-aware inference and calibration
  • Interpretability and explanation as interaction mechanisms
  • Feedback and preference modeling under interaction
  • Robustness, adaptation, and personalization over time
  • Evaluation metrics beyond standalone model accuracy that capture collaborative performance
  • Abstention policies and safety constraints
Benchmarks & Applications

Real-world deployments and use cases where interaction is essential, including:

  • Healthcare and medicine (e.g., clinical decision support, AI-assisted diagnosis)
  • Scientific discovery and AI for science
  • Recommendation systems
  • Entertainment and creative arts
  • Embodied agents and robotics
  • Personalization and preference elicitation
  • Scenarios involving partial information or underspecified prompts
  • Interactive benchmarks, evaluation protocols, and harnesses
  • Responsible deployment: auditing, transparency, and governance

Submission Details

We invite submissions that explore the topic of Human–AI Interaction across theory, algorithms, and practice.

Format & Tracks
  • Extended abstracts of up to 4 pages (excluding references).
  • Accepted papers will be presented as in-person posters. Top submissions will be selected for spotlight oral presentations.
  • There are no formal proceedings. Accepted papers will be publicly listed on the workshop website unless authors opt out for non-archival presentation.
  • We also welcome submissions of previously accepted conference/journal papers (published within the past year). These will not undergo the standard review process but will be evaluated by Area Chairs based on relevance to the workshop.
  • Only unpublished work will be considered for workshop proceedings.
Important Dates
Call for Papers March 22, 2026
Submission Deadline April 24, 2026
Reviewing Period April 26 – May 10, 2026
Notification of Acceptance May 15, 2026
Workshop Date July 2026 (at ICML 2026, Seoul, Korea)
Review Process
  • All submissions will be reviewed via OpenReview.
  • We enforce strict conflict-of-interest policies: reviewers cannot assess submissions from anyone who (1) has been a colleague within the same organization in the past 3 years, (2) has co-authored publications with them in the past 3 years, or (3) is currently at the same institution.
  • Reviewers are recruited from a wide variety of institutions and training levels to ensure fairness.
Submission Link
Submissions will be handled via OpenReview. The submission portal link will be announced when the call for papers opens on March 22, 2026.
